You are on the right track, but there is a technical distinction between a "Poster Image" and a "Lazy-Loaded Swap" that often trips people up.

If your code currently looks like a standard <video poster="image.jpg"> tag, you haven't actually bypassed the SEO penalty yet. Here is why, and how the "Thumbnail Solution" fixes it.

The Problem: Standard "Poster" Tags
Even if you have a static poster image, most browsers (and Google's bots) will see the <source> tag and immediately start a "partial download" of the video to check its metadata (duration, dimensions, etc.).

The Penalty: Google’s LCP (Largest Contentful Paint) often counts the video as the "main element." If the browser is busy negotiating a 10MB stream, your "Time to Interactive" score drops.

The Solution: The "Data-Src" Swap
The "Thumbnail Solution" (or true Lazy-Loading) involves hiding the video URL from the browser until the page is already "Stable" (finished loading).

Initial State: The HTML contains an <img> tag (your 200KB Jacksonville skyline). Google sees this, sees it loads instantly, and gives you an A+ for speed.

The Trigger: A tiny script waits for the window.onload event (meaning the rest of your site is fully functional).

The Swap: The script then injects the video URL into the background. The user sees the static image suddenly "come to life" and start moving.

How to check if you've actually done this
Open your website Insure-it and look at the Network Tab in your browser's inspect tool:

Fail: If the video file starts downloading at the exact same time as your logo and CSS.

Success: If the video file doesn't show up in the list until 1–2 seconds after the text is already visible.

Is it worth the hassle?
The guy you quoted is right about 100MB, but he's being a bit of a "performance purist" regarding 10MB.

For 10MB: A standard poster tag with preload="none" is usually "fine" for most local businesses.

For a Software Consultant: Since your site is a portfolio of your technical skill, implementing the JavaScript Swap proves you understand high-end web optimization. It’s a "flex" that shows you care about the user's data and speed.

Pro Tip: If you want the EverBank sign to look as sharp as possible, the "Thumbnail Solution" is actually your best friend. You can use a very high-res WebP image as the thumbnail (where the text is perfectly clear) and let the video be the "movement" in the background.
